Frequently Asked Questions

How to Subscribe to an IPO? +
  • Step 1: Login to your respective service provider.
  • Step 2: Click on the IPO button.
  • Step 3: Select the IPO you want to bid and enter the relevant details.
  • Step 4: Your subscription will be completed once you make the payment or give permission.
Should I buy an IPO first day? +
It depends on the company's fundamentals and market conditions. Some IPOs might give you good returns on the first day, but others could drop in value. Research and understand the risks before making a decision.
How do you know if an IPO is good? +
A good IPO is usually backed by strong fundamentals, a solid business model, and a growth strategy. Analyzing the company's financial health, industry potential, and management team can help assess the quality of the IPO.
How to check IPO start date? +
You can check the IPO start date on the company's website, through your broker, or on financial news websites. The start date is usually announced a few days before the IPO opens for subscription.
What is IPO issue size? +
The issue size refers to the total value of the IPO, calculated by multiplying the number of shares offered by the offer price. It indicates the amount of capital the company aims to raise.
How to sell IPO shares? +
IPO shares can be sold through your brokerage account once the shares are listed on the stock exchange. You can place a sell order based on the current market price.
Can I cancel my IPO application? +
Yes, you can cancel your IPO application before the allotment process begins. Check with your broker for the cancellation process and deadlines.
How many lots should I apply for in an IPO? +
The number of lots you should apply for depends on your investment goals and risk appetite. Keep in mind that applying for multiple lots does not guarantee full allotment, especially in oversubscribed IPOs.
